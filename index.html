Event Details

Mobile Application Design Contest 2015

The HKUST Mobile Application Design Contest (MAD contest) is open to the HKUST undergraduate/postgraduate students and alumni.

Come Along! Share your innovative, MAD ideas in the contest!!

Introduction

The MAD 2015 is the second launch of this contest. Absorping the successful experience in the 2014 contest, we continue to offer opportunity for the talent teams to showcase their high quality mobile application programs.

The special features in this year include - We broaden the software platform to include Android and iOS, catering for the Android and Apple lovers. Alternatively, you are also welcome to develop a hybrid application to run on both giant players' smartphones.

Contest Details

Organizer
The Department of Computer Science and Engineering of the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST).
Registration
Team structure: 2-4 members + at most 1 HKUST Alumus as advisor. Click registration link for the details.
Submission of proposal
After registration, Interested teams need to submit a 2-page proposal via proposal link. Shortlisted teams will be announced in 31st Dec 2015.
Submission of the application programs
Click Application Upload link for the details.
On the Contest Day
The contest will be held at the end of January in 2015 in the HKUST where the teams will showcase and live demonstrate their finished product in two rounds. 1st Round, teams will brief their products and promotional posters to the judges. then 5 teams will be selected to enter the final 2nd round to compete for the championship.
Note
Should your application program require any special equipment (phone docks for example) for the demonstration, you need to contact us at ustac@cse.ust.hk before registration so that we could evaluate to see whether we could support it.

PROPOSAL

Proposal Submission (Deadline 21st December 2015)

Click here for: Proposal Guideline

Page
Your proposal should contain a maximum of 2 pages.
Content
Put down your names and HKUST email addresses in the proposal.
File Size
Your proposal should be less than or equal to 2MB in size. Please contact us for submissions larger than 2MB.
File Format
Your proposal must be in either the MS Winword or the Adobe PDF format, with extension being doc, or docx, or pdf.
Others
  • You can submit your 2-page proposal for as many times as you want on or before the deadline. Only the last submission will be evaluated.
  • Should you require any special equipment (phone docks for example) for the program demonstration, you need to contact us at ustac@cse.ust.hk first, we need to evaluate to see whether we could support it.

FAQ

Q1. Can a team join the contest using an existing mobile application project, or they need to join using brand new code?

The application code does not have to be a brand new, but the team will have to present the new code they develop for the contest.

Q2. Is the team advisor complusory for a team?

Team advisor is optional. If you find an advisor (must be HKUST alumnus), then base on mutual arrangement between your team and the potential advisor, you could have him/her joined your team and advises your work. Remember to resgister your team and fill out the information of the team advisor if your team has one. If you find an advisor after your team has registered, please write us to update the team information.

Q3. Can an a HKUST alumnus be an advisor, if he/she works at HKUST.

As long as there is no conflict of interest (i.e. not taking part in oraganizing the contest, etc) any HKUST alumnus could be an advisor, but it will be better if the advisor is from the industry so that you will have a chance to gain the real-world experience.

Q4. Are the rules of the contest available?

Yes, they are available at here. Should any modification being made to the rules in the future, the modified set of rules will be available at the "What's new" page.

Q5. Are there guidelines for writing the 2-page proposal?

Yes, they are available at here. Should any modification being made to the guidelines in the future, the modified set of guidelines will be available at the "What's new" page.
